How To Play

Farming Simulator 20, part of the long-running Farming Simulator series, is a game that immerses players in the agricultural world, offering them a chance to manage their farm, operate heavy machinery, and cultivate various crops. The game's depth can be overwhelming for newcomers, but with a strategic approach and careful planning, players can cultivate a thriving farm.

When you first start your farming journey, it's crucial to familiarize yourself with the controls and the layout of your farm. Take some time to explore the map, noting the locations of important places such as the shop, grain silos, and your fields. Understanding where everything is located will save you a lot of time later on.

One of the first things you should do is to learn how to drive and operate the different types of machinery available. Each piece of equipment, from tractors to harvesters, has its specific use and learning how to maneuver them efficiently is key to running a successful farm. Practice makes perfect, so spend some time getting comfortable with each vehicle's handling and capabilities.

In Farming Simulator 20, time management is essential. You have a limited amount of daylight to work with, and planning your tasks efficiently will help maximize your productivity. Start by focusing on the most immediate tasks, such as preparing your fields for sowing. Tilling, sowing, fertilizing, and eventually harvesting your crops are the basic cycles you'll repeat. As you progress, you'll need to balance these tasks with animal care, should you choose to diversify your farm.

Crop selection is another important aspect of the game. Different crops have different growing times and market prices. Pay attention to the market trends and plant crops accordingly. Some crops may take longer to grow but will sell for a higher price, while others might be less profitable but can be harvested more frequently. Diversifying your crop selection can also help protect against market fluctuations and ensure a steady income.

Investing in the right equipment can make your farming operations more efficient. As you earn money from selling crops and livestock, consider upgrading your machinery. Larger tractors, more efficient harvesters, and better sowing machines can all save you time and increase your farm's output. However, it's also important not to overspend early on; make sure any purchases you make are justified by the potential increase in productivity.

Livestock is another avenue for income in Farming Simulator 20. Animals like cows, sheep, and pigs can provide a steady income stream through the products they produce, such as milk, wool, and meat. Taking care of animals requires daily attention, including feeding and ensuring they have clean water. While it adds complexity to the game, livestock management can be highly rewarding and provides diversification for your farming activities.

Efficiently managing your fields is also vital. Crop rotation and field maintenance, such as weeding and fertilizing, can lead to better yields. Pay attention to the needs of each crop type; some may require more fertilizer or different types of care. Keeping your fields in top condition will ensure that your harvests are as profitable as possible.

Another tip is to utilize the game's helper feature. Helpers can be hired to automate tasks such as sowing, harvesting, or transporting goods. This allows you to focus on other aspects of your farm or to manage multiple operations simultaneously. While hiring helpers costs money, the time they save you can often lead to greater overall efficiency and profitability.

Storage management is another key aspect of the game. Properly storing your harvested crops until market prices are favorable can lead to increased profits. Keep an eye on the market and sell your goods when prices are high. Similarly, buying seeds and other supplies when prices are low can reduce your operating costs.

Lastly, patience is a virtue in Farming Simulator 20. Success won't come overnight, and the game rewards careful planning and strategic thinking. Take your time to build up your farm, invest wisely, and don't rush into decisions. With time, you'll learn the intricacies of the game and develop a farming empire that is both profitable and enjoyable to manage.

By following these tips and strategies, players can enhance their Farming Simulator 20 experience. The game is about understanding the agricultural process, making smart investments, and enjoying the serene pace of farm life. With dedication and smart management, your farm will flourish, and you'll enjoy the fruits of your labor both literally and figuratively.
